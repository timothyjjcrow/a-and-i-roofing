Torch Down Roofing

Torch down roofing, also known as modified bitumen roofing, is a popular choice for flat or low-slope commercial and residential roofs. It consists of multiple layers of asphalt-based material, reinforced with polyester or fiberglass, that are heat-welded (torched) together to create a seamless, waterproof membrane.

A&I Roofing specializes in the expert installation and repair of torch down roofing systems. These roofs are known for their exceptional durability, weather resistance, and longevity, making them an excellent investment for protecting your property.

Excellent waterproofing capabilities
Highly durable and puncture-resistant
Resistant to UV rays and extreme weather
Long lifespan (20+ years)
Suitable for flat and low-slope roofs
Relatively low maintenance

Torch Down Roofing Systems

Understanding the types of modified bitumen roofing we offer

SBS (Styrene-Butadiene-Styrene) Modified Bitumen

SBS modified bitumen offers excellent flexibility and elasticity, even in cold temperatures. It has strong waterproofing properties and is highly resistant to weathering and punctures. Often installed in multiple layers.

  • Superior flexibility
  • Excellent cold weather performance
  • Strong adhesion

APP (Atactic Polypropylene) Modified Bitumen

APP modified bitumen provides enhanced UV resistance and durability, with a "plastic-like" quality that gives it a higher melting point. It is often chosen for its ease of application and long-term performance in sunny climates.

  • High UV resistance
  • Good heat tolerance
  • Durable surface

Multi-Ply Systems

For maximum protection and redundancy, we often recommend multi-ply torch down systems. These involve layering base sheets and cap sheets to create an exceptionally robust and long-lasting waterproof barrier.

  • Enhanced durability
  • Redundant waterproofing
  • Extended lifespan

Reflective Cap Sheets (Cool Roof)

To improve energy efficiency, torch down systems can be finished with reflective cap sheets. These "cool roof" surfaces reflect solar radiation, reducing heat absorption and lowering building cooling costs.

  • Energy savings
  • Reduced urban heat island effect
  • Extended roof life

Our Torch Down Installation Process

Ensuring a quality, long-lasting flat roof system

1

Consultation & Roof Assessment

We start with a detailed inspection of your existing flat or low-slope roof, discussing your needs, concerns, and budget to recommend the optimal torch down solution.

2

Surface Preparation

The roof surface is thoroughly cleaned and prepared. This may involve removing old roofing material, repairing the substrate, and ensuring a smooth, dry surface for adhesion.

3

Installation of Base Layers & Insulation (if needed)

A base sheet or insulation board is mechanically fastened or adhered to the roof deck. This provides a stable foundation for the torch down membrane.

4

Application of Torch Down Membrane

Rolls of modified bitumen are carefully unrolled and heat-welded to the underlying layer and to each other using a propane torch. Our experienced technicians ensure proper adhesion and seamless overlaps.

5

Detail Work & Flashing

Special attention is given to details like roof edges, drains, vents, and penetrations. Custom flashing is installed and torched to ensure these vulnerable areas are completely waterproof.

6

Final Inspection & Cleanup

A thorough final inspection is conducted to guarantee quality and adherence to specifications. We then ensure a complete cleanup of the site, removing all debris.
